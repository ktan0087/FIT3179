The Air We Breathe

Death Risk by Factor, 2019


Air pollution is a top-three risk factor for premature death and health issues globally, alongside smoking and high blood pressure, leading to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases. What are the factors that contribute to air pollution?

Who Experiences The Most Significant Impact of Air Pollution?


Air pollution contribute to 11.65% of deaths globally.

We can see that the most polluted countries are in Asia and Africa. Air pollution is more prevalent in poor and middle-income countries for two basic reasons. First, because they rely on solid fuels for cooking, low-income countries frequently have high levels of indoor pollution. Second, when countries industrialise and move from low to medium income status, outdoor air pollution tends to rise.

Top 10 Countries with highest PM2.5 levels (µg/m³)


PM2.5 refers to tiny airborne particles with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or smaller, which can have adverse health effects when inhaled into the lungs and are a major air pollution concern.

How GDP Is Associated With Air Pollution?


There is no clear correlation between a country's GDP per capita and its pollution levels. While some rapidly developing nations are currently facing increased pollution levels, others have managed to decrease hazardous substances through effective policies and regulations.
