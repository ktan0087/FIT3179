The Air We Breathe

Air pollution is a top-three risk factor for premature death and health issues globally, alongside smoking and high blood pressure, leading to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases. Reducing air pollution is vital for improving public health.
Who experiences the most significant impact of air pollution?
From the map above, we can see that the most polluted countries are in Asia and Africa. Air pollution is more prevalent in poor and middle-income countries for two basic reasons. First, because they rely on solid fuels for cooking, low-income countries frequently have high levels of indoor pollution. Second, when countries industrialise and move from low to medium income status, outdoor air pollution tends to rise.
